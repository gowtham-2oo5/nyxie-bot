==Description==
Green Hero is the first character developed in Heroes Battlegrounds, based on Izuku Midoriya, and is generally considered the easiest to use. Green Hero is a free character, serving as the default choice for new players. Green Hero possesses decent combo potential and a powerful ultimate when utilized effectively. Players can master this character to unlock Mastered Green Hero.
Achieving Mastered Green Hero requires gaining mastery. Mastery points are earned by defeating opponents while playing as Green Hero (0.50%), completing quests from the Daily Quest Giver (4-5%), and defeating The Prototype (5-13%).
== Pros and Cons==
===Pros===
* Can deal somewhat high damage.
* The Ultimate ability can inflict significant damage against both passive and aggressive playstyles.
* Players have ample time to execute combos on grounded opponents.
* Hyper grants hyper armor during its animation.
* Several moves can extend combos: Hyper (Air Variant), Focus Shot (Air Variant), and Blitz.
* Hyper (Air Variant) deals increased damage based on the user's height.
* Maximum Overdrive, offers excellent mobility options with OVERCHARGE (9 tiles), Cosmic Smash (12 tiles), and 1,000,000% (37 tiles).
* Following the Overkick rework, all base moves became unblockable.
* Opponents hit by Cosmic Smash cannot use ragdoll cancel to escape the move's effect.
* Hyper (Air Variant) stuns long enough to allow for a follow-up downslam, enabling potential triple downslam combos.
* Blitz can hit opponents out of ragdoll state, useful for initiating moves like Hyper or Focus Shot on opponents who were previously grounded.
* The Full Cowling passive allows for significant Ultimate bar gain upon kills, with minimal recoil damage taken by the player.
* Both Hyper and Focus Shot deal high amounts of damage to players.
* Hyper can be used as a prediction tool for predicting front dashes, due to it having super-armor.
===Cons===
* Green Hero's Awakening, Maximum Overdrive, is predictable and easily countered by experienced opponents.
* Hyper and Overkick have telegraphed startups, making them easy to predict and counter.
* The reworked Overkick animation is highly predictable.
* 1,000,000% lacks invincibility frames (I-frames) during its startup, meaning the user can be interrupted, cancelling the move entirely and wasting the Ultimate activation for its duration.
* Cosmic Smash is punishable if blocked.
* OVERCHARGE is difficult to aim accurately, cannot hit grounded opponents and is vulnerable to interruption during its startup phase.
** OVERCHARGE also has an unreliable hitbox.
* Overkicks prominent visual effects can obscure the player's view, potentially causing misses.
* Successful combos often depend on baiting out the opponent's evasive maneuvers. A failed bait can leave the player vulnerable to counter-attack.
* Focus Shot, while unblockable close up, is blockable at farther distances.
* The base moveset is very basic and does not have much room for creative combos, and does not have much combo potential.

==Description==
Hero Slayer is the second character released in Heroes Battlegrounds, based on Chizome Akaguro (also known as Hero Killer Stain). He is the first villain character added to the game. Hero Slayer is relatively easy to learn but challenging to master, featuring a moveset capable of executing deadly combos despite its longer cooldowns. This character can be mastered to unlock Mastered Hero Slayer.
Achieving Mastered Hero Slayer requires gaining mastery. Mastery points are earned by defeating opponents while playing as Hero Slayer (0.50%), completing quests from the Daily Quest button (4-5%), and defeating The Prototype (5-13%).
==Pros and Cons==
===Pros===
* Thousand Slashes or Triple Dagger Throw can substitute for a side dash if the M1 attack range is insufficient.
* Curdle Pierce and Thousand Slashes can cancel a forward dash if timed correctly.
* Blackout Clutch grants invincibility frames (I-frames), making it useful against multiple opponents (teamers).
* The Ultimate Mode, Conviction, is highly effective against teamers.
* High combo potential, allowing for freestyle gameplay.
* Beginner-friendly; relatively easy character to pick up initially.
* Can stun an opponent upon the second activation of Curdle Pierce by applying the "Blood Curdle" status (requires landing the first hit).
* Thousand Slashes and Triple Dagger Throw are effective combo extenders due to their speed and strong stun properties.
* Blackout Clutch provides temporary safety against teamers, granting I-Frames upon landing the move.
* Curdle Pierce is a strong tool for countering or initiating aggression due to its hyper armor.
* Triple Dagger Throw can extend combos off Blackout Clutch when followed by a side dash.
* Possesses one of the fastest Ultimate activation animations currently in the game.
* Capable of dealing high damage with True Combos (approximately 45-60%).
* Conviction (Ultimate) moves have very low cooldowns.
* Can consistently extend combos after catching an opponent with an Interaction With Objects (IWO) attack (unless used after the fourth M1).
* Capable of performing relatively easy One-Shot combos with proper timing.
* Effective character against The Prototype boss due to high Ultimate move damage and the ability to gain ultimate charge with Indomitable Aura.
* Crimson Parry has an insanely long counter window out of every counter in the game, making it incredibly easy to fall for if you are impatient.
* Curdle Pierce is an effectively good combo starter.
* Even if Curdle Pierce is missed, it doesn't have enough endlag to prevent you from blocking afterwards.
===Cons===
* May cause performance issues (lag) on lower-end devices.
* Most moves are punishable if missed or blocked.
* Difficult to fully master (player dependent).
* Utilizing advanced techniques requires significant practice and precise timing.
* The forward dash has a vulnerable hitbox for a few frames.
* Curdle Pierce has significant endlag, often leading to punishment if missed.
* A basic combo (Thousand Slashes > Triple Dagger Throw > Blackout Clutch) can deal around 50% damage but is predictable.
* Conviction has no actual mobility moves to catch runners.
* Attempting the Triple Dagger Throw wakeup tech on the ragdolled opponent only grants the user 4th M1 instead.

== SPLIT ICE ==
Category:Characters
== Description==
Split Ice is a free-to-play character in Heroes Battlegrounds, based on Shoto Todoroki from My Hero Academia. He is the fourth character added to the game. This character excels in close-range and mid-range combat, possessing strong combo potential. Players can master this character to unlock Mastered Split Ice.
Achieving Mastered Split Ice requires gaining mastery. Mastery points are earned by defeating opponents while playing as Split Ice (0.50%), completing quests from the Mastery UI (4-5%), and defeating The Prototype boss (5-13%).
==Pros & Cons==
=== Pros ===
* Frost Burst has a medium-to-large area of effect (it's slightly inconsistent hitbox can sometimes provide a minor advantage to the user).
* Ice Javelin features a ranged variant, activated by using the skill when no opponent is in the user's immediate melee range.
* Frost Burst is an excellent tool for finishing off low-health opponents.
* Capable of a high-damage (approximately 50%) true combo using only two moves and M1 attacks.
* Frost Counter, if landed, stuns the hit opponent(s) for 4 seconds (up to 6 seconds with the passive-boosted version).
* Successfully landing Frost Counter allows the user to inflict significantly more damage.
* Frost Counter can counter any non-ultimate move in the game.
* Sub Zero has four distinct variants, offering versatility.
* The Glacial Resonance passive, which enhances freeze effects, is useful against multiple opponents (teamers).
* The Grab and Uppercut Variants of Sub Zero grant invincibility frames (I-frames).
* Sub Zero's Uppercut Variant can be timed to hit opponents just before they use an evasive maneuver, leading to high-damage true combos.
* Possesses simple combos that can be developed into deadly sequences with proper execution.
* Can deal substantial damage when played effectively.
* Ice Javelin provides an M1 reset, though it reduces subsequent M1 damage to approximately 1% per hit.
* Frost Counter is an effective combo starter when successfully landed.
* Frost Burst has a high finisher threshold (14%) and its finisher activates immediately.
* Heatwave Strike grants Super Armor during its animation.
* Blazing Soul has a long duration (26 seconds), which can be used to drain an opponent's ultimate bar if they are caught in the cutscene.
* Due to Burning Flames (both the activation and ultimate moves) dealing significant amount of damage makes Split Ice a very good choice against the The Prototype boss.
** This is also useful for gaining mastery for this character.
* Ice Javelin (Ranged Variant) is a very good combo starter, due to being able to be used at a distance.
* The ultimate is very good for boss farming.
=== Cons ===
* Blazing Soul can be difficult to control and may hit unintended targets.
* Using the same Sub Zero variant multiple times consecutively incurs a longer cooldown.
* Sub Zero's Downslam and Ranged variants can be easily dodged with a ragdoll cancel.
* The character's basic M1 combo damage is relatively low.
* Opponents can perform a forward dash towards the user during the startup of Ice Javelin's ranged variant, leading to an easy punish.
* The hitbox for Ice Javelin's ranged variant is the projectile model itself, making it avoidable with a simple jump or side dash.
* The ranged variant of Sub Zero is considered one of its weaker options.
* Blazing Soul has the longest ultimate move cutscene in the game, which can be disruptive if an unintended target is hit.
* Both melee and ranged versions of Ice Javelin severely reduce subsequent M1 damage.
* Opponents with fast reactions or low latency can evade Frost Counter with a side dash.
* The Burning Flames ultimate has only one dedicated mobility move (Blazing Soul), and its other moves have limited range.
* Sub Zero’s ranged variant is very inaccurate
* The base moveset is very basic and does not have much room for creative combos, and does not have much combo potential.

==Description==
Explosion Hero is the 7th character added to Heroes Battlegrounds (10th character overall, including Private Server exclusives). This character is based on Katsuki Bakugo from the series My Hero Academia. His abilities revolve around his Quirk, which allows him to create explosions from his nitroglycerin-like sweat.
Explosion Hero is unique as the first character to possess two distinct Ultimate Modes: Fury Ignition and TENTH TORCH. To activate TENTH TORCH, the user must deal sufficient damage while in the Fury Ignition state (approximately 85% of an opponent's health bar or equivalent damage dealt to any target) and then use the fourth ability slot within the Fury Ignition moveset. This multi-stage ultimate system contributes to Explosion Hero being considered a highly effective yet the strongest choice against the The Prototype boss.
==Pros and Cons ==
===Pros===
* Possesses very simple yet high-damage combos.
* The character offers significant mix-up potential and combo opportunities, particularly with his first and second base moves.
* Features two Ultimate forms, allowing the user to enter two separate invincibility-frame (I-frame) states, potentially avoiding high-damage enemy attacks.
* Exploding Star pauses the Ultimate bar drain for its entire cinematic duration, enabling the user to maximize their ultimate mode uptime and safely wait for other ability cooldowns.
* Ground Cannon can extend combos into every other base move, including Blast Catapult.
* Fury Ignition is effective against multiple opponents (teamers).
* All moves in the Fury Ignition ultimate have low cooldowns.
* Every base move has a finisher, facilitating easier activation of the character's passives.
* Fury Ignition provides high mobility with the Eruption Dive ability.
* Eruption Dive has a large Area of Effect (AoE), making it effective for damaging multiple opponents.
* THE TENTH TORCH, when all its moves and the transformation sequence are landed, can deal astonishing damage (up to approximately 348%).
* Due to it's higher damage output of both Ultimate activations and their subsequent moves makes Explosion Hero a stronger choice for defeating the The Prototype boss.
* BLAST AWAY is almost a guaranteed kill if landed after three M1s against an opponent with no evasive options remaining.
* BLAST AWAY grants I-Frames during its animation, even if it does not hit a target.
* All base moves can combo-extend into each other.
* NUKE preserves the user's killstreak despite the self-destruct mechanic.
* Due to having two ultimates, using both on teamers is very effective and rewarding.
* Since you do not have to use Powered Shot and Ground Cannon's second use right away, you can use the second use as another move in a combo.
* His passive allows for more reliable combos with moves like Explosion Combo.
* Powered Shot's second use has a large amount of range.
* Every move can be used after Explosion Combo.
===Cons===
* Blast Catapult can sometimes launch targets too far for a reliable follow-up, or occasionally fail to launch them at all.
* The base moveset arguably has one of the lowest total damage outputs per individual hit among all characters.
* The first use of Powered Shot has recoil, which can cause the user to miss follow-up attacks (e.g., a frontal dash). The second use of Powered Shot also has specific timing requirements.
* If the user does not deal enough damage to unlock THE TENTH TORCH, Fury Ignition effectively only has three offensive ultimate moves.
* The character initially struggles with consistent, high-damage combos as it lacks true combo extenders in its base kit, relying on opponents wasting their evasive maneuvers.
* Although TENTH TORCH deals immense damage, its duration is shorter than other ultimates, and its moves can be punished if misused.
* The startup animation of NUKE does not have I-Frames, allowing opponents to interrupt it.
* BLITZ KICK is easily punishable if used without setup (raw) or if it misses.
* Ground Cannons timer was decreased, forcing you to use it quickly.

== Description ==
Warp Portal is a character in Heroes Battlegrounds, based on the villain Kurogiri from My Hero Academia. He was the 6th character added to the game (7th including Huge Lady). As a master of spatial manipulation through his 'Warp Gate' quirk, Warp Portal's moveset revolves around teleportation, creating portals for offensive and defensive maneuvers, and disorienting opponents with unpredictable attacks.
The Warp Portal moveset is an unlockable character. It is obtained by defeating The Prototype boss, with an obtainment chance ranging from 7% to 100% based on the player's accumulated Pity. Pity is gained when a player is among the top three damage dealers in a successful The Prototype defeat but does not receive Warp Portal. The amount of pity gained depends on placement: 3rd place grants 1% pity, 2nd place grants 2% pity, and 1st place grants 3% pity. Warp Portal is awarded if the random number generated (shown in a notification) is lower than the player's current pity percentage.

* If timed correctly, Portal Cascade can hit a ragdolled (mainly after an uppercut) opponent, to do this you must do it immediately after or wait until they are within head height.

== HUMAN ARCHITECT ==
==Description==
Human Architect is the twelfth character released in Heroes Battlegrounds, based on the antagonist Kai Chisaki (Overhaul) from My Hero Academia.
His core gimmick is a highly adaptive and versatile moveset centered around the Conjure ability. Using other base moves (Renovation, Dirty Work, Stone Weaponry) before Conjure changes its properties, offering different offensive and combo-extending options. This rewards strategic sequencing of abilities. Furthermore, Human Architect possesses a unique, multi-stage awakening. The first stage, Flesh Chimera, enhances his abilities. By meeting specific conditions within this form, the user can then activate Final Fusion, a second awakening that temporarily transforms them into a giant, Kaiju-like monster with immense power, before transitioning into a final, powered-up humanoid form with a devastating new moveset.
==Pros and Cons==
===Pros ===
* Offer an excellent combo potential, with most moves flowing seamlessly into one another, especially with the different variants of Conjure.
* Renovation disables a random move from the opponent's own moveset, creating a significant disadvantage for them in the battle.
* The pillar created by Dirty Work can be used for an I.W.O. interaction.
* Dirty Works endlag can be oddly deceptive, allowing for an easy block if the opponent punishes slightly late.
* Conjure (after Renovation) is an easy follow-up to the said move.
* Conjure (after Dirty Work) has one of the highest finisher execution thresholds for a base move, finishing at 20% health.
* Using Conjure after Stone Weaponry offers a seamless and deadly combo.
* Calamity has a fast startup, and can be followed up with Creation Field seamlessly.
* During the Kaiju form (achieved via Final Fusion), the user is seemingly invulnerable to most attacks, with Split Ice's Blazing Soul and Decaying Hatred's Reflective Counter being an exception. This, along with the powerful moves in the final awakened state, makes the character's ultimate state exceptionally strong.
* Doubtlessly, Kaiju form is the character against teamers, since use of Final Fusion, and then CREATE, can provide invulnerability (if not counting moves that make you attack unwillingly, e.g. BUTCHER) for a really long time.
* The Kaiju form lasts an entire ultimate, which means that if someone uses ultimate while you use Final Fusion, by the time it finishes, their ultimate will have likely run out.
* BUTCHER (Aerial Variant) can be used to catch an opponent from a distance.
* RUPTURE covers a massive amount of range in front of the user.
===Cons ===
* His base moveset possesses below-average damage by itself, relying more on creative combos to deal maximum damage.
* Stone Weaponry has a very low finisher execution threshold (8%).
* Conjure (Neutral Variant) has extreme endlag if it is blocked or misses.
* The landing shockwave from Dirty Work can sometimes be easily countered by parry moves.
* The pillar created by Dirty Work deals reduced damage as an I.W.O. compared to standard map pillars.
* Disfigurations finisher does not pause the ultimate bar, potentially wasting time for the activation of Final Fusion.
* Activating the Kaiju form requires meeting two conditions: filling the DEATH gauge (a secondary ultimate meter) and then landing a finisher on another player, which can be difficult to set up. Using a Weakest Dummy may not grant the necessary finisher credit.

==Description==
The Strongest is a special character in Heroes Battlegrounds based on the immensely powerful sorcerer Satoru Gojo from the series Jujutsu Kaisen. True to his inspiration, this character possesses overwhelmingly potent abilities, focusing on high burst damage, unique defensive capabilities and devastating area-of-effect attacks in his awakened state.
== Ways to Obtain ==
There are two methods to obtain The Strongest:
1. The Strongest NPC (Daily Chance): Players can interact with the Gojo NPC once every 24 hours.
* If the NPC responds with "Better luck in 24 hours," the player did not receive the character and must try again the next day.
* If the NPC says "YES YES YOU ARE... at least for 24 hours", the player has successfully unlocked the character for a temporary 24-hour period, usable in both public and private servers. The chance of obtaining the character this way is extremely low (approximately 1 in 1,350,000, or 0.000074%).
2. Private Server Plus Gamepass: Purchasing the Private Server Plus gamepass for 499 Robux grants permanent access to The Strongest, but only within the owner's private server.
== Keep in Mind ==
* If obtained via the NPC, your character will be switched back to Green Hero (or your default character) if you are playing on a public server when the 24-hour duration expires, and you cannot switch back without obtaining it again.
* If The Prototype boss spawns while you are playing as The Strongest, your character will be automatically switched to Green Hero.
==Pros and Cons==
===Pros===
* All moves deal exceptionally high, often one-shot, damage.
* M1 attacks (The Strongest Punches) are incredibly powerful, capable of defeating opponents in a single combo.
* Infinity provides a near-permanent counter, reflecting most incoming damage back to the attacker.
* Possesses a couple of strong mobility options, especially Blue (if missed).
* Ultimate moves (Purple, Red, Decapitate) are devastating and can eliminate multiple opponents in a single blow.
* Unlimited Void offers powerful crowd control by immobilizing enemies caught in its activation.
* Most moves gains massive amounts of ult if used on people/dummies.
===Cons===
* Extremely low chance of obtaining from the NPC, making it highly inaccessible for most players through that method.
* Permanent access via gamepass is restricted to private servers only.
* Infinity can be bypassed by specific counter moves (e.g., Hero Slayer's Crimson Parry).
* Forced character switch if The Prototype spawns.
* If obtained via NPC, access is temporary (24 hours).

Huge Lady is a character in Heroes Battlegrounds based on the Pro Hero Mt. Lady (Yu Takeyama) from My Hero Academia. This character is exclusive to Private Servers that have the Private Server+ (PS+) gamepass active.

==Description==
Dullahan, also known as Pumpkin King, was a Halloween 2025 Boss in Heroes Battlegrounds a player can play the game with upon a certain condition.
To become Dullahan, you need to wait for the Pumpkin Moon to rise (Every 75 Minutes in Public Servers), which is seen by skybox turning orange. After it does, you need to collect four pieces of him: his armor, his head, his greatsword and his horse.
Once Dullahan dies, the system will note: "Dullahan's ghost rests... USERNAME, the Pumpkin King, has died! They managed to gather (Kills during Dullahan) Souls!"
== Pros and Cons==
=== Pros ===
* All of Dullahan's moves are uninterruptable when start-up is not interrupted.
* False Sun has auto aim and deals a lot of damage.
* The minions from using the Rise of Pumpkins can interrupt other players attempts to attack you.
=== Cons ===
* Lack of i-Frames after move end leaves Dullahan vulnerable.
* Some moves are punishable after they end.

==Trivia/Notes==
* If you try to perform an emote with Dullahan used, a notification will pop up and note: "Sorry, you cannot emote as Dullahan. Sorry, too many bugs."
* When gaining a 10 Killstreak on Dullahan, you get a special emote called Horseman's Call.
* Dullahan's moves can be customized via VIP Effect Color.
* The minions uses Winged Assassin's M1 sound effect when they attack the player.
* False Sun may be a reference to Demon Slayer, as Hinokami Kagura is known as the False Sun by the fanbase. It also may be a reference to False Rainbow.
** For some, False Sun might refer to Cruel Sun.
* This character was removed after the Christmas update, due to being Halloween-exclusive.
** Could be brought back in the 2026 Halloween event.
* Dullahan has much more Health Points than The Prototype, according to the first surviving NUKE without critical health drop.
** Theoretically, Dullahan's Health Points might be '1,000'.
* The best character to use in order to find the pieces to access this character may be Mastered Green Hero due to its awakening, as its move can dash you forward at accelerating speeds. 
** Much better alternative is Full Throttle, due to the later having the moves that push player into a set direction both in base and awakening.
* Soul Harvest can drain a small portion of the target's ultimate meter, both if the target is in ultimate mode or not.

ULTIMATE/AWAKENING: ==Ultimate Bar==
Whenever you deal damage to someone (Player, Weakest Dummy, Leaderboard #1-3 Avatars), you gain charge in your Ultimate Bar, and when it's filled to 100%, you can activate your Ultimate Mode by pressing G on keyboard, D-Pad up on controller or tapping the Ultimate Bar itself on mobile. When switching characters, the Ultimate Bar will be depleted by 1/4, and switching constantly can bring it to complete zero.

Upon entering Ultimate Mode, a cutscene will play (for most characters) on your character along with exclusive music attached to it, with some cutscenes dealing damage to players in the surrounding area.
